Project Brainstorming

Every project has to start with an idea!

Posted on September 15, 2021

Here are some of my current ideas for the Physics Personal Project:

  • Computer Science
    • The main idea I have related to computer science is creating a simulator or physics engine. In particular, I could connect this to my next idea and create a physics engine that simulates a rocket launch based on different modifications and conditions.
  • Space Flight/Astronautics
    • This idea is more hands-on, and the final product would involve building a physical rocket myself. I would have to answer questions such as:
      • How do rockets work?
      • How do you launch a rocket successfully (connecting to Newton's third law, thrust, and other physics equations)?
  • Astrophysics
    • It would be more difficult to relate this topic to the course material and create an experiment that I could conduct, but astrophysics interests me. I am not too sure about what exactly I would focus on, but I could talk about the different characteristics of planets in space, such as their rotation (conservation of angular momentum) causing their orbit.
  • Robotics/Some Sort of Machine
    • Combining computer science with a more hands-on project, I want to create something that can predict the trajectory and landing spot of a ball using a camera. This idea is pretty ambitious, and I would need to research the viability of building something capable of this, but it nicely connects the course with my passions.
  • Wireless Charging
    • Wireless charging is another hands-on project that nicely connects course material with real-life technology. Wireless charging relies on magnetic fields and transfers energy to your phone without a wire (duh). For the final product of this project, I would build a wireless charger for my phone.
  • The Physics of Flight
    • This idea is similar to rocketry, and I would be building many planes to test the factors affecting flight. This idea interests me because I can conduct experiments and go through multiple models to improve the design of my aircraft to optimize distance. There are a lot of things I can fiddle around with, and the flexibility is appealing.
