Measuring Success

Overall, I am quite happy with how the project turned out. In the end, I was actually able to create a physics engine and demonstrate it by simulating some cool scenarios (found at the last minute of the video). More importantly, I learned about how computers simulate physics, improving my knowledge in both physics and math. It was very convenient learning about impulse in class and then using it in my engine a few days later. I am most proud of my video — I love how it turned out, and I think it reflected my learning and work in this Personal Physics Project very well. I also learned to animate using Manim, which is something I have been meaning to do for a while, and it was great being able to tie it into something productive :)

I think it's appropriate to provide the code used to create all the animations in the video, which can be found here.

Areas for Improvement

I wanted to implement my physics engine for all polygons and ellipses, but after spending several frustrating days on it, I decided my math and physics knowledge was not good enough to upgrade my current engine of rectangles and polygons.

Additionally, I really lacked in the blogging department for the primary research after adding my first iteration of the physics engine. After adding a new component, it felt so boring to go back and write about what I did (I also dislike writing), so I didn't do it. Next time, I should at least make an effort to record videos of each additional component and upload it to the blog to record my progress.

Finally, there were two minor errors in my video, which I could have eliminated if I had finished earlier and sent it to more peers (although the video you are seeing is the fourth revision).

Concluding Words

I don't see much benefit for making my reflection long — I'm proud of my project, and my video reflects my learning and final product very well. There's always room for improvement, and I don't think my work with this physics engine ends with SPH4UE-A. I'm genuinely passionate about this project, and in the future, I want to extend it to support polygons and ellipses. Who knows, maybe the next time you check this blog, I'll have posted a new update about new shapes!

I'm happy with how much I learned over the past four months, and I hope you've learned something from my journey as well. ☺︎
